# Synthetic Housing Executive Briefing Script

## Slide 1: Executive Summary
Good morning. This briefing reviews synthetic housing utilization, vacancy trends, and data quality risks across fictional installations. The purpose is to show how recurring data analysis can support leadership decisions around housing capacity, building utilization, and reporting accuracy.

## Slide 2: Key Metrics
The dashboard tracks total beds, occupied beds, vacant beds, average net use percentage, buildings below the utilization target, and open work orders. These metrics help leaders understand both capacity and risk.

## Slide 3: Main Findings
First, vacancy is concentrated in a smaller group of installations. Second, buildings with lower condition scores tend to have weaker utilization. Third, training housing shows seasonal utilization movement, which means monthly trend analysis is more useful than a one-time snapshot.

## Slide 4: Data Quality Risks
The raw dataset included common reporting issues such as missing building numbers, duplicate records, invalid occupancy values, inconsistent installation names, and incorrect calculated utilization percentages. These issues can distort leadership reporting if they are not corrected before dashboard refresh.

## Slide 5: Recommendations
The recommendation is to create a monthly utilization exception report, standardize naming conventions, use recurring SQL validation checks, and prioritize review of buildings with high vacancy, low condition scores, and high work order volume.
